What is 3D Printing?

3D printing, also known as additive manufacturing, is a process of creating three-dimensional objects from digital designs. Unlike traditional manufacturing that removes material, 3D printing builds objects layer by layer, allowing for complex geometries and customization. In Nepal, this technology is revolutionizing prototyping, product development, and small-scale manufacturing.

Common 3D Printing Applications in Nepal

  • Rapid prototyping for product development and engineering
  • Custom parts and components for machinery and equipment
  • Educational models and teaching aids
  • Medical devices and prosthetics
  • Jewelry and fashion accessories
  • Architectural models and scale representations
  • Replacement parts for legacy equipment
  • Art and sculpture creation

FDM vs Resin Printing

FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling)

Uses thermoplastic filaments like PLA, ABS, and PETG. Ideal for functional prototypes, durable parts, and large objects. Cost-effective and widely available materials.

Resin Printing

Uses liquid photopolymer resins cured by UV light. Produces high-detail, smooth surfaces perfect for jewelry, dental applications, and detailed miniatures.

Material Guide

Material Best Use Notes
PLA Beginner-friendly, biodegradable, good for prototypes and non-functional parts
PETG Durable, chemical resistant, ideal for mechanical parts and outdoor use
ABS/ASA Strong, heat-resistant, suitable for engineering applications
TPU Flexible, rubber-like, perfect for gaskets, seals, and wearable items
Nylon High strength, abrasion resistant, great for functional prototypes
Resin (Standard) High detail, smooth finish, for precise models and figurines
Resin (Tough) Durable and impact-resistant, for functional parts needing strength
Resin (Flexible) Soft and bendable, for applications requiring flexibility
